# Harlem Grown — Organization Overview

## Mission
To inspire youth to lead healthy and ambitious lives through mentorship and hands-on
education in urban farming, sustainability, and nutrition.

## Founded
2011 by Tony Hillery with one school partnership and one urban farm in Central Harlem.

## Scale (Current)
- 14 urban agriculture facilities (soil farms, hydroponic greenhouses, school gardens, mushroom chamber)
- 18,000+ youth served annually
- 40+ staff, 5,000+ volunteers per year
- Located 122nd–152nd Streets, Central Harlem, Manhattan
- $5M+ annual fundraising goal

## Key Stats for Pitching
- 90% of kids who grow food try a new vegetable for the first time
- 80% want to eat that vegetable again
- Free programming for all participants
- Produce distributed free to community members weekly
- 70% of corporate visitors who take a farm tour become partners

## Founder Story
Tony Hillery started Harlem Grown after volunteering at PS 175 in Harlem and seeing
children who had never seen a vegetable grow. He transformed abandoned lots into thriving
urban farms. Today the organization is a cornerstone of Central Harlem.
