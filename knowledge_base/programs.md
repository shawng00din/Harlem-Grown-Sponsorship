# Harlem Grown Programs

## 1. School Partnerships (Intensive)
- **Scale:** 6 partner schools in Central Harlem
- **Model:** Mentor-in-residence visits each school weekly; students tend beds on-site and visit HG farms
- **Duration:** Full academic year, September–June
- **Students:** K-8, approximately 1,200 students across all partner schools
- **What sponsors fund:** Mentor salaries, school garden materials, curriculum development
- **Naming opportunity:** "The [Company] Garden at PS 175" — permanent signage at a school
- **Best for:** Companies with strong K-12 education focus (Archetype B, C, E)

## 2. Summer Camp
- **Scale:** 7-week intensive program, 30 youth per cohort, two cohorts per summer
- **Ages:** 8–14
- **Cost to participant:** Free
- **Curriculum:** Urban farming, nutrition education, cooking, science, leadership
- **What sponsors fund:** Full cohort sponsorship ($25K–$50K names a cohort)
- **Naming opportunity:** "[Company] Summer Camp Cohort"
- **Best for:** Food companies, health companies, consumer brands (Archetype A, B, D)

## 3. Saturday Leadership Program
- **Scale:** 20 students, April–October
- **Ages:** 14–18
- **Focus:** Farm work, community service, leadership development, college prep
- **What sponsors fund:** Program operating costs, stipends for student leaders
- **Naming opportunity:** "[Company] Saturday Leadership Program"
- **Best for:** Financial services, professional services, workforce-focused companies (Archetype C)

## 4. Farm Tours & Workshops
- **Scale:** 100+ schools and groups annually
- **Format:** 60–90 minute guided tour with planting or harvest activity
- **Corporate version:** Available for 6–50 employees; includes student-led tour and farm-to-table lunch
- **Cost:** No cost for schools; corporate groups may make a donation
- **Conversion rate to partnership:** ~70% of corporate visitors become partners within 12 months
- **Best for:** First ask for almost every prospect — this is HG's most powerful sales tool

## 5. Workforce Development
- **Scale:** 20–30 emerging adults per year, ages 18–24
- **Population:** At-risk youth, justice-involved individuals, young adults without post-secondary plans
- **Skills:** Agricultural certifications, food service, landscaping, environmental education
- **Outcomes:** 75% employment placement within 6 months
- **What sponsors fund:** Staff, certifications, stipends
- **Best for:** Financial services, insurance, tech companies with workforce pipeline focus (Archetype C, E)

## 6. Community Food Distribution
- **Scale:** Thousands of pounds of fresh produce distributed weekly
- **Format:** Free produce stand at each farm location; open to all community members
- **Volunteer opportunity:** Available to corporate groups for the produce stand
- **Best for:** Food companies, healthcare companies, community-focused brands (Archetype A, B)

## 7. Mobile Teaching Kitchen
- **Scale:** Visits 30+ community sites per year
- **Format:** Nutrition education, cooking demonstrations, healthy eating workshops
- **Population:** Families, seniors, community members who can't travel to farms
- **What sponsors fund:** Kitchen vehicle maintenance, curriculum, staff
- **Best for:** Healthcare, food companies, wellness brands (Archetype A, B)
