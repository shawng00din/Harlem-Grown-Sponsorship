# Harlem Grown Sponsorship Tiers

## Platinum Partner — $100,000+
- Program naming rights (one school partnership for 1 year)
- Annual gala presenting sponsor
- 4 corporate volunteer days (up to 50 staff each)
- Quarterly impact reports with photos and stories
- Co-branded communications and press release
- CEO recognition at all major events
- Farm naming opportunity (e.g., "The [Company] Farm at 128th Street")

## Gold Partner — $50,000
- Named sponsor of one Summer Camp cohort
- 2 corporate volunteer days (up to 30 staff each)
- Semi-annual impact reports
- Gala table (10 seats)
- Co-branded social media features (2 posts per quarter)

## Silver Partner — $25,000
- Named sponsor of the Saturday Leadership Program
- 1 corporate volunteer day (up to 20 staff)
- Annual impact report
- 4 gala tickets
- Website and newsletter recognition

## Community Partner — $10,000
- Named sponsor of a farm site for 1 year (signage, social media)
- 1 group farm tour + harvest experience (up to 20 people)
- 2 gala tickets
- Social media recognition (quarterly)

## First Step — Site Visit (No commitment required)
- 90-minute guided farm tour for 6–10 corporate decision-makers
- Includes student-led tour of 1–2 farm sites
- Harvest activity
- Farm-to-table lunch prepared by HG staff and students
- No dollar commitment required — this is an experience
- Conversion rate to partnership: approximately 70%

---

## Notes for Development Team

The site visit is almost always the right first ask for a cold prospect.
Dollar asks in a cold letter almost never work. The farm converts people.
Bring them to the farm first. The check comes after they've met the students.

For prospects with a board connection or existing warm relationship,
you can skip the site visit and go straight to a Silver or Gold ask.

For major corporations (Fortune 500 with established foundations),
the Platinum conversation can begin at first meeting — but still lead
with the volunteer day as the entry point.
