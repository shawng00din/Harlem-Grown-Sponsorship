# Harlem Grown — Company Archetype Profiles

Identify the best-fit archetype after scoring. The archetype determines the
lead angle for outreach and the tone of the introduction letter.

---

## Archetype A — The Mission Soulmate

**Who they are:** Food/beverage brand, food justice foundation, CPG company with
a sustainability-first identity. Their core business overlaps directly with what
Harlem Grown does.

**Signals:** Food or ag sector, nutrition/food justice language in CSR, B Corp
or 1% for Planet member, high Dim 1 (Food & Nutrition) score.

**Lead angle:**
> "You're already doing this work — we're the NYC chapter."

**Letter tone:** Peer-to-peer, enthusiastic, values-forward. Speak as a fellow
practitioner, not a grant supplicant. Reference shared mission directly.

---

## Archetype B — The Community Health Champion

**Who they are:** Hospital system, health insurance company, wellness brand,
pharmacy chain. They fund health outcomes and see nutrition/food access as
preventive medicine.

**Signals:** Healthcare or wellness sector, mention of social determinants of
health, existing food access or nutrition programs, high Dim 2 + Dim 3 scores.

**Lead angle:**
> "We're practicing preventive medicine — 18,000 kids a year."

**Letter tone:** Clinical warmth, outcomes-focused. Use their language around
health equity and social determinants. Lead with impact data.

---

## Archetype C — The Resilient Financier

**Who they are:** Bank, insurance company, private equity firm, or financial
services company with Community Reinvestment Act (CRA) obligations or a named
community foundation.

**Signals:** Financial sector, CRA mentions, named foundation or community
investment program, high Dim 6 (Giving Capacity) + Dim 4 (NYC proximity).

**Lead angle:**
> "Urban resilience starts with healthy communities and educated youth."

**Letter tone:** Formal, precise, ROI-minded but mission-present. Acknowledge
their CRA framework. Frame HG as a high-leverage community investment.

---

## Archetype D — The Purpose-Driven Consumer Brand

**Who they are:** DTC brand, B Corp, 1% for Planet member, or retail company
that has made sustainability and community a core brand identity.

**Signals:** B Corp certification, strong Dim 3 + Dim 7 scores, cause-marketing
history, consumer-facing sustainability narrative.

**Lead angle:**
> "We're the urban farm version of what your brand stands for."

**Letter tone:** Collaborative, brand-aware, cause-marketing framing. Reference
their campaigns or certifications. Position HG as the authentic NYC story behind
their brand promise.

---

## Archetype E — The Tech Neighbor

**Who they are:** NYC-based tech company, startup, or platform company with a
STEM education or workforce development CSR focus.

**Signals:** Tech sector, STEM education programs, future workforce language,
NYC headquarters or significant NYC presence, high Dim 5 (volunteer appetite).

**Lead angle:**
> "STEM + sustainability. Your future workforce grows up on these farms."

**Letter tone:** Energetic, data-curious, future-focused. Use their own
innovation/impact language. Frame the farm as hands-on STEM education.

---

## Archetype F — The Real Estate Transformer

**Who they are:** NYC developer, construction company, or real estate investment
firm with community benefit agreements or neighborhood transformation commitments.

**Signals:** Real estate or construction sector, community benefit language,
Harlem or upper Manhattan projects, high Dim 4 (NYC proximity) score.

**Lead angle:**
> "We transform abandoned lots too — just with seeds instead of steel."

**Letter tone:** Neighborhood-rooted, transformation narrative. Reference specific
NYC neighborhoods or projects. Frame HG as a community infrastructure partner.

---
